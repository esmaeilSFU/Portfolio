[CON]CREATE ; A Digital Fabrication Solution

A Geometrical Approach to Mitigate High-tech Machinery Requirement for Construction of Irregular Concrete Structures

Project Overview

The pavilion showcases a new fabrication method that shifts construction complexity from high-tech equipment to computational design strategies. Instead of using robotic arms, large CNC milling machines, or specialized molds, the project employs affordable and widely available 2D CNC laser cutting to create formworks and structural components. This method allows for the fabrication of highly intricate designs while keeping costs and production time low. The final structure serves as proof of concept, illustrating how computationally optimized geometries can be realized with low-tech tools.

Key Innovations :

Low-Tech Fabrication

Uses 2D CNC laser cutting instead of robotic arms or complex machinery, reducing costs, energy use, and material waste while ensuring precision.

Computational Geometry Approach

Optimizes compression-only structures using force-density relaxation, minimizing the need for post-processing and ensuring structural efficiency.

Modular & Reusable Design

Features dry joints for easy disassembly and reassembly, promoting sustainability by reducing waste and enabling material reuse.

Sustainable Construction

Eliminates foam molds and excessive material use, optimizing concrete consumption and lowering environmental impact.

Methodology :

Form-Finding & Optimization

Uses force-driven computational networks to design a compression-only structure, eliminating the need for external reinforcements.

Fabrication Strategy

2D CNC laser cutting produces interlocking formwork and reinforcement elements, enabling precise construction without high-tech machinery.

Material Innovation

Utilizes lightweight, fiber-reinforced self-compacting concrete, ensuring strength, durability, and compatibility with the fabrication method.

Assembly Process

Designed for easy assembly by amateur workers, eliminating the need for heavy machinery or scaffolding.
